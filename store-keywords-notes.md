# Store Keywords Notes

Use the same honest positioning across the website, store descriptions, and social posts.

## Core phrases

- Explain Yourself Game
- photo party game
- icebreaker game
- conversation starter game
- iPhone and Android app
- Peter Euphosin

## Recommended consistent sentence patterns

- Explain Yourself Game is a photo party game app for iPhone and Android.
- Spin, reveal a real photo, and explain what is happening.
- Great for parties, road trips, icebreakers, and conversation starters.
- Created by Peter Euphosin.

## Website guidance

- Keep the current privacy language straightforward and specific.
- Use "Explain Yourself Game" as the primary product name in the title, description, and hero copy.
- Mention "photo party game" naturally near the top of the page.
- Use "icebreaker" and "conversation starter" sparingly and only in natural context.

## App Store / Google Play guidance

- Keep the first sentence aligned with the website:
  Explain Yourself Game is a photo party game app for iPhone and Android.
- Reuse the core gameplay sentence:
  Spin, reveal a real photo, and explain what is happening.
- Keep "photo party game" as the main phrase.
- Use "icebreaker game" and "conversation starter game" in supporting copy, not as repeated labels.

## Social guidance

- Lead with the product name first: Explain Yourself Game.
- Follow with the simple gameplay loop.
- Mention the creator name only where it feels natural:
  Created by Peter Euphosin.
- Avoid overloading every post with every keyword.

## Do not do

- Do not keyword-stuff product descriptions.
- Do not make claims about rankings, installs, or reviews without evidence.
- Do not rewrite every sentence to chase search terms at the expense of clarity.
